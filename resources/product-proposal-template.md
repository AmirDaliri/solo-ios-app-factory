# Product Proposal Template

Fill every section. Keep it to roughly one screen. Tag demand/competition claims [FACT] or [ASSUMPTION].

## [App working name]

**One-liner.** What it does and for whom, in one sentence a stranger understands.

**Target user.** The specific person, not "everyone". Their role/situation and the recurring moment they hit the pain.

**The pain.** What hurts today and how they cope now (the workaround you're replacing). Tag the evidence.

**The wedge.** Why this wins where incumbents don't — the defensible angle, not the feature list.

**Why now / why this builder.** What makes it a good fit for a solo senior iOS dev right now (platform capability, gap, on-device edge).

**Monetization (headline).** Primary model + rough price. One line; detail lives in the monetization section.

**Riskiest assumption.** The single belief that, if wrong, kills the product. State it as a falsifiable sentence.

**Cheapest validation.** The least-effort test that could disprove the riskiest assumption before any code.

**Scope sanity.** One sentence confirming the MVP is a 2–4 week solo build, or flagging that it isn't.

## If this is an execution-play candidate (Lane B)

When the winner came through Lane B rather than scoring ≥80, the proposal must make the execution case explicit. Add:

**The fragmented field.** The top 2–3 incumbents and why none is a loved leader (ratings, staleness, bloat, price), with the repeated user complaint that is the opening. Tag [FACT].

**The one narrow workflow.** The single job the MVP beats incumbents on (not "everything, but nicer"). Name it concretely.

**The execution advantage.** The specific edge on that workflow — simpler flow, faster capture, better offline, tighter Apple integration, clearer pricing, fewer features — not vague "better UX."

**What you will not try to beat.** The incumbents' real moat (data, integrations, brand). Conceding it keeps scope honest.

**Distribution reality.** How this reaches users without out-spending incumbents on marketing — and, if the user is a domain tourist, how they reach the audience at all.
